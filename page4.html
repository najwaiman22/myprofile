Masak Lemak Cili Api Ayam

‚è±Ô∏è 40 mins
üë• 4 servings
üå∂Ô∏è Spicy

Rich, creamy, and fiery! Traditional Malay curry with tender chicken in coconut milk and bird's eye chili.

Ingredients:

  • 500g chicken pieces (cut into chunks)
  • 400ml coconut milk
  • 10-15 cili api (bird's eye chili)
  • 2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 2 stalks lemongrass (bruised)
  • 5 shallots (sliced)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tomato (quartered)
  • Salt to taste
  • Cooking oil

Steps:

  1. Heat oil in a pot. Saut√© shallots, garlic, and lemongrass until fragrant.
  2. Add turmeric powder and stir for 1 minute.
  3. Add chicken pieces and cook until slightly browned.
  4. Pour in coconut milk and bring to a gentle boil.
  5. Add cili api and tomato. Simmer for 20 minutes until chicken is tender.
  6. Season with salt to taste. Serve hot with rice!

Dendeng Daging

‚è±Ô∏è 2 hours
üë• 6 servings
üå∂Ô∏è Sweet & Spicy

Slow-cooked sweet and savory beef jerky, caramelized to perfection with a hint of spice.

Ingredients:

  • 500g beef (thinly sliced)
  • 100g brown sugar
  • 3 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 2 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp fennel seeds
  • 5 shallots (blended)
  • 3 cloves garlic (blended)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • Cooking oil for deep frying

Steps:

  1. Marinate beef with blended shallots, garlic, coriander, fennel, and salt for 2 hours.
  2. Heat a pan with a little oil. Add marinated beef and cook over low heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add brown sugar and tamarind paste. Continue cooking until liquid evaporates.
  4. Once dry, heat oil in a separate pan for deep frying.
  5. Fry the beef until crispy and caramelized.
  6. Drain excess oil and let cool. Store in airtight container.

Sambal Udang

‚è±Ô∏è 25 mins
üë• 4 servings
üå∂Ô∏è Very Spicy

Succulent prawns stir-fried in homemade fiery sambal with hints of tamarind sweetness.

Ingredients:

  • 500g fresh prawns (cleaned and deveined)
  • 15 dried chilies (soaked)
  • 1 tbsp belacan (shrimp paste)
  • 3 tbsp tamarind juice
  • 2 tbsp palm sugar
  • 2 tomatoes (cut into wedges)
  • 5 shallots
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • Salt to taste
  • Cooking oil

Steps:

  1. Blend soaked chilies, shallots, garlic, and belacan into a smooth paste.
  2. Heat oil in a wok. Fry the paste over medium heat until fragrant and oil separates.
  3. Add tamarind juice and palm sugar. Stir well.
  4. Add tomatoes and cook until soft.
  5. Add prawns and stir-fry until cooked (about 3-5 minutes).
  6. Season with salt to taste. Serve immediately with steamed rice!
